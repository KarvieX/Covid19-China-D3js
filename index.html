We designed two graphs to visualize the spread of the conaravirus breakout among all provinces in China, and conducted a time series analysis by week.
The two visualizations for our data -
  1. A map-based heapmap to show how the disease spread geographically in China over time.
  2. A radical stack bar chart to overview the exact number of confirmed / death / recovered cases in all provinces.
Encoding Menthods
The primary purpose of the visualization is to understand how and where the infectious virus breakout and spread to, so the geographical info showing the trend of the disease breakout is encoded using map in this case, and the most attractive element, color, is used to encode the amount of confirmed cases, to show the severe situation of virus and its impact on each area.
We onceconsidered to use pyramids or normal ranked bar chart to show the case numbers for the right part of the data vis. However, the radical one could have a metaphor that all provinces actually connnect with each other and are part of the destiny of China together.

Interactivity Techniques
We mainly use Hover and Button switch as interactivity in our data vis, to enable exploratory while trying to eliminate unnecessary visual distractions.

Our Development Process and Discovered Insights

Develop Process

We started from choosing a data anlaysis and vis topic based on a recent hot topic. We followed the process of:

  • Found suitable datasets and cleaned the proper dataset.
  • Played with the dataset - conducting basic exploratory analysis.
  • Agreed on the most important story to show through our vis - how the disease was spread and how it impacted each area.
  • Designed the vis (initial sketching) and iterated the design according to peer feedback.
  • Decided the encoding channels: Color to show the impact by representing the scale of case numbers; Geographical location to show its spread route.
  • Coded out the design and adjusted code chunks after combining each sections.

Some Difficult Time

  • Visualization decision: We argued on what kind of charts could better represent our story. Some map vis could show piecharts or barcharts to represent more data, we've spent time on simplifying our visualization, making it clear and self-expressive without requiring much user thinking. Among 5-6 options we used two seperate but complementary charts visualizing the geographical spread and numbercal increase of the disease.
  • D3.js coding: We've spent a very long time translating design into code, espceially the button switch part that listens to the hover interaction and update the whole vis. We were very desperate when we spent over 10h without figuring out how to realize it.
  • Coding cooperation: It's been very difficult to split the work/coding, and we found it really hard to sync up and combine.

Team Work Assignment

We splited our work by every part of the vis. On average, each member spent 10h to investigate corresponding techniques.

Some Data Insights

  • Outbreak spread: The coronavirus mainly spread from Wuhan to its adjacent provinces as well as closely connected provinces. For example, Guangdong has a large amount of high-speed railways connected with Wuhan.
  • Death rate: Although the confirmed cases seems terrifying, the death rate of coronavirus remained as low as 2%.
  • Government control: Over 1 month, the scope of coronavirus outbreak was controlled within the southeastern China, suggesting that the government took immediate actions to control the disease.
  • Population factors: Although the southwestern China seems to have less cases, it may not suggest a better prevention as there were less population.
